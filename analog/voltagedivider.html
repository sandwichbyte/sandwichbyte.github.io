When $a \ne 0$, there are two solutions to \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\) and they are $$x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac} \over 2a}.$$
When there is no load current, the output voltage is \(V_{out, no load}=\frac{R_{2}}{R_{2}+R_{1}}V_{in}\). The Thevenin voltage source of the divider is the open circuit voltage at the output, namely \(V_{Th}=V_{out, no load}=\frac{R_{2}}{R_{2}+R_{1}}V_{in}\) and the Thevenin resistance is \(R_{1} // R_{2} = \frac{R_{1}R_{2}}{R_{1}+R_{2}}\).
